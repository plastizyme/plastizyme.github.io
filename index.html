About

Plastizyme was founded by a scientist based at UNC Chapel Hill, driven by a passion to push the boundaries of science in tackling plastic pollution. Recognizing that PETase enzymes are now advanced enough for real-world use, we set out to bring enzyme-driven plastic degradation into homes for the first time.

The Problem

Plastic waste takes centuries to break down, overwhelming landfills and oceans. Current recycling efforts fail to keep up, leaving the world searching for a better solution.

The Opportunity

Breakthroughs in PETase enzymes offer a game-changing way to degrade plastic efficiently—breaking it down at the molecular level instead of relying on industrial recycling.

PETase

What are Plastic-Degrading Enzymes?

PETase is a naturally occurring enzyme that breaks down PET plastic into its base components, allowing for true biodegradation rather than accumulation in landfills.
